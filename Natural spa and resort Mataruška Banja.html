Natural spa and resort Mataruška Banja

In this project, I reimagine the nostalgic allure of vintage postcards through a contemporary lens, focusing on a once-popular spa and resort now shrouded in chaos and neglect. My photographs serve as a juxtaposition to the idyllic scenes captured in the original postcards, transforming their serene beauty into a haunting portrayal of decay.

Each new postcard reflects the haunting remnants of a vibrant past—a once-bustling sanctuary for relaxation and escape. Where there were smiling faces and serene landscapes, now lies an unsettling stillness: crumbling walls, overgrown gardens, and empty fountain. This stark contrast invites viewers to confront the passage of time and the inevitable decline of even the most cherished spaces.

Through this exploration, I seek to evoke a sense of nostalgia for what once was while highlighting the beauty found in ruin. By presenting these chaotic scenes in a format reminiscent of traditional postcards, I encourage viewers to reflect on memory, loss, and the layers of history embedded in these abandoned places.

Ultimately, my work challenges the viewer to consider how we document and remember spaces that have lost their former glory, prompting a dialogue about the delicate balance between beauty and decay.
